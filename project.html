Summary

For my first project as a UX Designer Apprentice at Bloc my task was to design a cloud storage application and brand that would rethink the cloud storage market by targeting the problems people face today.

DELIVERABLES: Interface Design for an App
MY ROLE: UX Design, UI Design, Brand Identity
TOOLS: Draw.io, Figma, Google form, UsabilityHub, InVision

Problem

What features does the platform need to fit into the already crowded cloud storage and organization market?

Solution

The final product DataBox, a cloud storage platform that would allow users to create documents, upload files, folders, Images, photos, and videos. It’s different from its competitors in that it’s more personable and focused on backing up content on cloud. The product is accessible on both desktop and mobile devices.

Research And Discovery

Goals

1. Perform a through and substantial research.

2. Discover the app inside and out.

3. Anticipate all the problems can possibly solved with creating app.

4. Come up with a thorough list of questions that can be answered through research.

Competitive Analysis

My competitive analysis revealed that a new competitor entering the cloud storage and organization app market should:

- Adopt competitors’ strengths and improve on their weaknesses

- Introduce new features.

- Be generous with file size

- Go strong on backup features

User Surveys

Before diving into any design tools, it was important to gather feedback from targeted Data Box Users. As a designer, I wanted to learn about their current habits when browsing content online.What apps are they using? This would help prioritize the roadmap for Data Box. Look at the Survey Questions

Have you ever used or currently store any data or files using a Cloud Based Storage service such as Dropbox or iCloud?

More than 70% of survey participants are familiar with cloud storage technology.

Select which type of data or files you currently store, or would consider storing using a Cloud Based Storage service?

More than 60% of users use it for backups, storing their files, documents, photos, videos etc.

User Personas

Create realistic personas based on survey results. Represent distinct use cases through each persona. Make sure that the personas are diverse enough and not too similar from each other. Based on the data from the survey and from follow up I have created two distinct personas to embody the full range of target audience.

Look at the User Personas

Wendy

Product Manager

Age: 25-35 / Gender: Female

Introvert, hard working, doer, busy.

“Giving and receiving feedback is key to learning.”

Motivation

As being product manager she creates and shares data with her colleagues and clients. Mainly shares project trackers to keep everyone informed and on track during working process. Also finding new ideas on web and downloading them for future references is important task. Also she follows design trends through pinterest. As a part of her job profile she collaborate with others in real time through slack and shares documents with others. She is not a social media person so privacy and security is very important aspect for her. She keeps her file private and encrypted.

Frustration

Moving files from different locations.
Sharing limitations within email option.

Emily

Designer/Photographer

Age: 30-40 / Gender: Female

Extrovert, Doer, Busy and Social

“I am always looking for ways to be more efficient.”

Motivation

Working in the museum feels like she is stuck in the old world of technology. In her day to day work she uses dropbox to share documents primarily images, But occasionally use it to store her video recordings. She uses a pinterest to get inspiration from the web. Sometimes she uses bookmarks to save content like articles, images for future purposes. Also in her free time she works as a photographer. This profession involves lot of data sharing and storing, so currently she is experimenting between google drive and dropbox for storage options, since she mainly store images. Organization and storage options are very important for her.

Frustration

It is safe to save data locally than on cloud platform Using cloud platform in workplace is hardNot enough storage options.

Visual Design

Branding

I kicked off the logo design by creating a mental map of the word “storage”. Jotting down related words helped me think of themes that could potentially inform the logo. From there, I began to sketch out potential logos using a pen and paper. This was the most foreign process to me, but I really tried to let my ideas flow. I aimed to create a scalable logo that was bold, but friendly.


Data Box which I thought perfectly captured the idea associated with backing up any type data in the cloud. Providing customizable organization and collaboration features. We genuinely care about helping users to be more productive and have peace of mind. Knowing that all their precious memories and creative projects are in a private and secure place. Our goal is to give user experience comparable to having a reliable and friendly personal assistant.
